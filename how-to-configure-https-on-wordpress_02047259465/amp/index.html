How to Configure HTTPS on a WordPress Site with GoDaddy SSL

How to Configure HTTPS on a WordPress Site with GoDaddy SSL In today's world of cybersecurity, it is essential to secure your website with HTTPS.

HTTPS encrypts data transmitted between the user's browser and the website, making it more challenging for attackers to intercept and steal sensitive information.

In this blog post, we will explore how to configure HTTPS on a WordPress site with GoDaddy SSL.



What is GoDaddy SSL?

GoDaddy SSL is a digital certificate that verifies the identity of a website and encrypts data transmitted between the user's browser and the website.

GoDaddy SSL certificates are widely recognized and trusted, making them an excellent choice for enhancing website security.

Step-by-Step Guide to Configure HTTPS on a WordPress Site with GoDaddy SSL

Step 1: Purchase a GoDaddy SSL Certificate To configure HTTPS on your WordPress site with GoDaddy SSL, you must first purchase a GoDaddy SSL certificate.

You can purchase a GoDaddy SSL certificate from the GoDaddy website or through your hosting provider.

Step 2: Install the GoDaddy SSL Certificate on Your Server After purchasing a GoDaddy SSL certificate, you must install it on your server.

The exact process for installing an SSL certificate may vary depending on your web host, but GoDaddy provides detailed instructions for installing their certificates on various web hosting platforms.

Step 3: Update Your WordPress Site's Settings After installing the SSL certificate, you must update your WordPress site's settings to use HTTPS instead of HTTP. To do this, log in to your WordPress site's dashboard and navigate to Settings > General.

Change both the WordPress Address (URL) and Site Address (URL) fields to use HTTPS instead of HTTP.

Step 4: Update Your WordPress Site's Content After updating your site's settings, you must update any internal links or resources to use HTTPS instead of HTTP.

You can use a plugin like "Better Search Replace" to update all instances of HTTP links to HTTPS automatically.

Step 5: Test Your HTTPS Configuration After configuring HTTPS on your WordPress site, you should test your site's HTTPS configuration to ensure that it is working correctly.

You can use a tool like the SSL Server Test to test your SSL certificate and ensure that your site is using HTTPS correctly.

Conclusion

Configuring HTTPS on your WordPress site with GoDaddy SSL is an essential step in enhancing your site's security and protecting your users' sensitive information.

By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can configure HTTPS on your WordPress site with GoDaddy SSL and ensure that your site is using HTTPS correctly.
